Concepts

Agricultural City

Cities that rely on agricultural surplus from surrounding hinterlands to generate division of labor and support advanced organizations.

Irrigated Land Density

Irrigated land appears in areas with gentle slopes; with adequate local precipitation or insufficient local precipitation but accessible rivers that can be utilized. If the slope is too steep, or higher than the river level, high-density irrigated land cannot appear. Particularly low-lying terrain is prone to flooding, which is unfavorable for agriculture, but the development of hydraulic technology can address this problem to some extent.

Hydraulic Technology

As mentioned above, it is primarily related to the ability to transform low-lying terrain into irrigated land.

Drainage Slope

Cities on flat land have lower transportation costs, but on excessively flat and low-lying land, they are easily threatened by floods.

Flood Risk

Low-lying topography has high flood risk. Additionally, the upstream watershed area is also a factor. If the watershed area is small, the risk of low-lying topography is also manageable.

Collection Distance

Collection costs include management and transportation, beyond distance considerations.

Methods

Using real topographic maps to analyze key variables. The analysis method is quite complex. Simplified to three variables: low-lying degree, irrigated land density, and collection distance.
